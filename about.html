About Us

Welcome! We are R2G2, an all-girls robotics team from Notre Dame Academy High School. We are an elective class, not a club, that meets every Friday for 2 hours after school. This is our second year participating in VEX Robotics and we are ecstatic for all the opportunities that will allow us to demonstrate what we are capable of. R2G2 has 27 members, divided into two teams: the A team and B team. R2G2 was made possible by the Northrop Grumman grant.

  • B TEAM

TEAM 3980A

Our A team consists of Seniors and Juniors who partake in R2G2. They have been involved with robotics at Notre Dame Academy since their freshman year. They are the base and more experienced team.
NDA began to offer Robotics 2 years ago.
18 members

TEAM 3890B

Our B Team is made up of our Freshman and Sophomore team members. The B team was founded on October 14, 2016 as a learning experience for our lower classmate robotics participants and they are excited to grow and learn in their experience in R2G2.

2014-2015 Season



Skyrise: 2014-2015


Game Details: VEX Skyrise is played by two alliances (Red and Blue), each alliance made up of two teams, on a 12'x12' square field. The game consists of a 15 second autonomous period followed by driver-controlled play for 1 minute and 45 seconds. The goal is to stack your colored cubes (22 red, 22 blue) in floor goals, on posts or on your skyrise, by owning posts, and by building your skyrise sections

2015-2016 Season



Nothing but Net: 2015-2016


VEX Nothing but Net is played by two allicances (red and blue), each alliance made up of two teams, on a 12'x12' square field the game consists of a 15 second autonomous period followed by driver-controlled play for 1 minute and 45 seconds. The goal of the game is to attain a higher score than the opposing alliance by scoring the balls and bonus balls (94 balls and 10 bonus balls) in your low and high goals, and by elevating robots in your climbing zone.

Viewpoint School - Clash in the Canyon

Prize: VEX Robotics Sportsmanship Award - October 17, 2015

The “Sportsmanship” award is presented to a team that has earned the respect and admiration of the volunteers and other teams at the event. This team is a model for all to follow and interacts with everyone in a positive, respectful manner in the spirit of friendly competition and cooperation. This award is judged during the event by teams, referees and volunteers.

Granada Hills Charter High School - Robodox Challenge 2015

Prize: VEX Robotics Create Award November 21, 2015

The “Create” award is earned by a team whose robot design incorporates a creative engineering solution to the design challenges of this year’s game. Attributes such as solid mechanical ability, unique design solutions and innovative approaches to playing the game will be taken into account by the judges looking for teams demonstrating a highly creative engineering design process.
