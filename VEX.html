A little bit about VEX

VEX Robotics Competition is the largest middle and high school robotics competition in the world. Standard matches are composed of two alliances of two teams each competing against each other. There is one robot and one driver per team playing for a limited amount of time. The games include an autonomous section in which the teams are autonomously drive their robots using programming skills. There are currently over 10,000 teams from 32 countries playing in 750 tournaments.

Us in VEX.

Notre Dame Academy has participated in the VEX competition program since January of 2015. We are made up of two all-girls teams.

  • 2015-2016 Season

2014-2015 Season aka Skyrise

VEX Skyrise is played by two alliances (Red and Blue), each alliance made up of two teams, on a 12'x12' square field. The game consists of a 15 second autonomous period followed by driver-controlled play for 1 minute and 45 seconds. The goal is to stack your colored cubes (22 red, 22 blue) in floor goals, on posts or on your skyrise, by owning posts, and by building your skyrise sections.

2015-2016 aka Nothing But Net

VEX Nothing but Net is played by two allicances (red and blue), each alliance made up of two teams, on a 12'x12' square field the game consists of a 15 second autonomous period followed by driver-controlled play for 1 minute and 45 seconds. The goal of the game is to attain a higher score than the opposing alliance by scoring the balls and bonus balls (94 balls and 10 bonus balls) in your low and high goals, and by elevating robots in your climbing zone.

Competitions and Awards

    Date: 17 October, 2015
    Prize: VEX Robotics Sportsmanship Award

    • The “Sportsmanship” award is presented to a team that has earned the respect and admiration of the volunteers and other teams at the event.   • Granada Hills Charter High School - Robodox Challenge 2015
