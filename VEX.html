VEX


Notre Dame Academy has participated in the VEX competition program since January of 2015. VEX Robotics Competition is the largest middle and high school robotics competition in the world. Standard matches are composed of two alliances of two teams each competing against each other. There is one robot and one driver per team playing for a limited amount of time. The games include an autonomous section in which the teams are autonomously drive their robots using programming skills. There are currently over 10,000 teams from 32 countries playing in 750 tournaments.




2014-2015 Season



Skyrise: 2014-2015


Game Details: VEX Skyrise is played by two alliances (Red and Blue), each alliance made up of two teams, on a 12'x12' square field. The game consists of a 15 second autonomous period followed by driver-controlled play for 1 minute and 45 seconds. The goal is to stack your colored cubes (22 red, 22 blue) in floor goals, on posts or on your skyrise, by owning posts, and by building your skyrise sections

2015-2016 Season



Nothing but Net: 2015-2016


VEX Nothing but Net is played by two allicances (red and blue), each alliance made up of two teams, on a 12'x12' square field the game consists of a 15 second autonomous period followed by driver-controlled play for 1 minute and 45 seconds. The goal of the game is to attain a higher score than the opposing alliance by scoring the balls and bonus balls (94 balls and 10 bonus balls) in your low and high goals, and by elevating robots in your climbing zone.

Viewpoint School - Clash in the Canyon

Prize: VEX Robotics Sportsmanship Award - October 17, 2015

The “Sportsmanship” award is presented to a team that has earned the respect and admiration of the volunteers and other teams at the event. This team is a model for all to follow and interacts with everyone in a positive, respectful manner in the spirit of friendly competition and cooperation. This award is judged during the event by teams, referees and volunteers.
